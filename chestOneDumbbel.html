Chest Single Dumbbell Workout

Single Dumbbell Chest Workout at Home. Try this workout with ONLY ONE DUMBBELL.

This workout can also be done with no bench needed.

This at Home Chest workout is great for training your upper, lower and mid-chest from home using only one dumbbell.

We have 7 exercises:

1. Single Dumbbell Double Floor Press

2. Single Dumbbell Close Floor Press

3. Single Dumbbell Decline Press

4. Single DB Floor Flys

5. Single DB Floor Press

6. Single DB Floor Flys
1. Single Dumbbell Double Floor Press (4 SETS / 12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 12 reps. Lightweight, do 20-30 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at both ends, then push up and down.

3. Keep knees bent while doing this.

4. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

5. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

6. Keep elbows in the same position throughout.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

2. Single Dumbbell Close Floor Press (4 SETS / 12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 12 reps. Lightweight, do 20-30 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at the centre then push up and down.

3. Keep knees bent while doing this.

4. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

5. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

6. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

3. Single Dumbbell Decline Press (4 SETS / 12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 12 reps. Lightweight, do 20-30 reps.

2. Start in a decline position and then grab both ends of the dumbbell. Push along the length of your legs.

3. Make sure your lower body is lifted up off the floor.

4. Make sure you're pushing the dumbbell up along the length of your legs not straight upward.

5. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

6. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

4. Single DB Floor Flys (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a moderate weight dumbbell for this. I used 30 lbs in this video.

2. Start in a decline position and then lift the dumbbell along your sides squeezing your chest at the top each rep.

3. Place the other arm on the ground with palms facing down.

4. Lift your arm from outwards towards the opposite side while squeezing your chest.

5. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

6. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

5. Single DB Floor Press (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate weight dumbbell for this. I used 30 lbs in this video.

2. Start by marking the centre with one hand and then go ahead and do a single arm floor press with the other.

3. Make sure you use your opposite arm as a marker and bring the dumbbell to it everytime.

4. Everytime you bring your arm down, avoid letting it rest on the floor. Keep the tension throughout.

5. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

6. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

6. Single DB Floor Flys (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate weight dumbbell for this. I used 30 lbs in this video.

2. Start by laying arm out straight to one side with palms facing up and then bring the dumbbell up to meet your other palm.

3. Make sure you're squeezing your chest on every rep before bringing the weight down.

4. Control your breathing (breath in when you push the weight up and out when you bring it down).

5. Make sure you use your opposite arm as a marker and bring the dumbbell to it everytime.

6. Make sure you only have a slight bend in your elbow and the arm is fully extended before bringing back up.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.
