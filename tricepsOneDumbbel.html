Tricep Single Dumbbell Workout

Single Dumbbell TRICEP Workout at Home. Try this Workout with ONE DUMBBELL only!

This TRICEP workout is great for training all the different Tricep Muscles.

This is a great single dumbbell workout for women and men.

We have 6 exercises:

1. Overhead DB Press

2. Single Tricep Extensions

3. High Diamond Pushup

4. Single Arm Overhead Press

5. Behind The Back Extensions

6. Single Arm Outward Extensions
1. Overhead DB Press (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8-12 reps. Lightweight do 15-20 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell over your head and lowering slowly until you feel the tension in your triceps.

3. Make sure you lower the dumbbell as far as your arms can. Be careful not to hurt yourself.

4. Control your breathing(Breath in when you bring up the weight. Breathe out when you lower).

5. Make sure you're lifting the weight all the way up before lowering. Avoid locking elbows.

6. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

2. Single Tricep Extensions (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Do as much as 20 reps per arm if your dumbbell is too light.

2. Start in a leaning position. Tuck arm to side and keep elbow locked in while extending your triceps.

3. Make sure you're kicking back the dumbbell without compromising the starting position of your body.

4. Control your breathing(Breath in when you bring up the weight. Breathe out when you lower).

5. Make sure you're extending the dumbbell all the way until your arm straightens.

3. High Diamond Pushup (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. The dumbbell weight doesn't matter.

2. Start by standing the dumbbell on it's side. Balance on it safely and then push up and down. Do it without the dumbbell for less of a challenge.

3. Make sure you're going all the way down before pushing back up.

4. Maintain a close distance between both feet.

5. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

4. Single Arm Overhead Press (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Do as much as 20 reps per arm if your dumbbell is too light.

2. Start in a kneeling position. Lower the dumbbell from one side down towards the other until you feel tension in your triceps.

3. Make sure you're not rushing through it. Lower the dumbbell slowly.

4. Place your inactive arm behind your back or on your lower abdomen for balance and suport.

5. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

5. Behind The Back Extensions (4 SETS / 12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8-12 reps. Lightweight do 15-20 reps.

2. Start by leaning forward. Hold a dumbbell behind your knees and extend backward.

3. Make sure you're extending your arm all the way until your elbows are almost straight.

4. Keep your elbows locked in and do not move them from the starting position as you kickback the dumbbell.

5. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

6. Single Arm Outward Extensions (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Do as much as 20 reps per arm if your dumbbell is too light.

2. Start in a leaning position. Flare elbow all the way out and extend arm all the way until you feel the tension.

3. Make sure you're extending your arm all the way until your elbows are almost straight.

4. Maintain the same elbow position throughout the entire exercise.

5. Maintain the same leaning position you start in throughout. Do not compromise it.

6. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.
