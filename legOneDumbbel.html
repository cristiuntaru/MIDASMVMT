Leg Single Dumbbell Workout

Single Dumbbell Leg Workout at Home. Try this workout with ONLY ONE DUMBBELL.

This leg workout is great for your quads, hamstrings, and glutes.

This is a great single dumbbell workout for women and men.

We have 7 exercises:

1. Single Leg DB Sumo Squats

2. Single DB Regular Squats

3. Single DB Calf Raises

4. Single DB Side Lunges

5. Single DB RDLs

6. Single DB Front Lunges

7. Single DB Reverse Lunges
1. Single Leg DB Sumo Squats (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8-12 reps. Lightweight, do 20-30 reps

2. Start in a wide stance and squat until you feel tension in your legs, then explosively back up.

3. Make sure to squeeze your glutes/butt at the top of every lift.

4. Make sure you aren't lifting the weight with your hands. They should only act as support for the weight.

5. Control your breathing (Breathe in when you lower the weights down and out when you bring the weights up).

6. Make sure to lower body to just below 90 degrees.

2. Single DB Regular Squats (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8-12 reps. Lightweight, do 20-30 reps.

2. Start in a neutral stance and squat until you feel tension in your legs, then explosively back up.

3. Make sure you squat until you're in a sitting position.

4. Make sure you aren't lifting the weight with your hands. They should only act as support for the weight.

5. Control your breathing (Breathe in when you lower the weights down and out when you bring the weights up).

6. Hold the dumbbell evenly so the weight is distributed properly.

3. Single DB Calf Raises (4 SETS / 20 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 20 reps. Lightweight, do 20-40 reps.

2. Start in a close stance and raise your calves until you feel tension and hold for one sec then back down.

3. Focus on the workout and maintain balance so you don't tip over.

4. Use a plate, a board, a book, or anything for increased efectiveness.

5. More balance is required when using the place. Keep that in mind.

4. Single DB Side Lunges (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/SIDE)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8-12 reps/leg. Lightweight, do 15-20 reps/leg.

2. Start at shoulder width and then ludge to one side until you feel tension in your hamstrings and back. Repeat for the other leg.

3. Lunge until you feel tension in your hamstrings.

4. Lunge to 90 degrees from one end to the other.

5. Single DB RDLs (4 SETS / 8-10 REPS)
TIPS:

1. Use a moderate dumbbell weight. Lightweight dumbbell? Do 20 reps/leg.

2. Start with the both feet close to each other. Lean forward letting the dumbbell down until you feel tension in your hamstrings muscles.

3. Flex and squeeze butt or glutes for more efectiveness.

4. Lean forward without using arms to lift. They should act as a hook only!

5. Lower weights until you feel your hamstrings resisting.

6. Aim to lower the dumbbell till your body is at least 90 degrees.

7. Focus on maintaining your balance and completing each rep slowly.

6. Single DB Front Lunges (3 SETS / 8-12 REPS/LEG)
TIPS:

1. Use a heavy dumbbell for 8-12 reps/leg. Use a lightweight for 15-20 reps/leg.

2. Start with the both feet close to each other. Lunge forward to a 90 degree angle and back. Repeat with other leg.

3. Keep your back straight and lunge forward till your knees are at 90 degrees with the floor.

4. Lunge back to the same starting position every time and then alternate and repeat with the other leg.

7. Single DB Reverse Lunges (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. Use a heavy dumbbell for 8-12 reps/leg. Use a lightweight for 20-30 reps/leg.

2. Start with the both feet close to each other. Lunge forward to a 90 degree angle and back. Repeat with other leg..

3. Keep your back straight and lunge forward till your knees are at 90 degrees with the floor.

4. Lunge back till you feel tension in your hamstrings alternate and repeat with other leg.
