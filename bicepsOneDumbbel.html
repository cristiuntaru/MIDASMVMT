Bicep Single Dumbbell Workout

Looking for a Single Dumbbell Bicep Workout at Home?

- Try this workout with only one dumbbell for the bicep muscles.

- This workout can be done at home, at the gym, or anywhere with no bench needed!

We have 7 exercises:

1. Single Bicep Curls

2. Single Hammer Curls

3. Single In x Out Curls

4. Double Grip Hammer Curls

5. Negatives

6. Hammer x Bicep Curls

7. Concentration Curls
1. Single Bicep Curls (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. If you have a lightweight dumbbell do more reps (12-15). If you have a heavyweight dumbbell do less reps (6-8).

2. Great for targetting the entire bicep for overall growth.

3. Make sure to let the weight all the way down before curling back up.

4. Curl the weight up without swinging your body.

5. Make sure you're currling all the way up.

6. Curl slowly to increase time under tension.

2. Single Hammer Curls (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. If you have a lightweight dumbbell do more reps (12-15). If you have a heavyweight dumbbell do less reps (6-8).

2. Use a hammer grip for this.

3. Fully curl the dumbbell up and down for the full range of motion.

4. Keep your elbows from flaring and maintain the same starting position throughout.

5. Great for targetting the outer region of the biceps and the forearms.

3. Single In x Out Curls (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. This is a hybrid workout - each rep is a combination of each exercise to make for one rep.

2. Use a lightweight dumbbell for this.

3. Maintain the same position for each workout in the hybrid.

4. This targets your inner and outer biceps.

5. Lower the weight all the way down for each before switching for the other.

6. Make sure you're curling until you feel your biceps squeeze, then all the way back down.

4. Double Grip Hammer Curls (4 SETS / 8-10 REPS)
TIPS:

1. Heavyweight dumbbell workout is recommended for this.

2. If you only have a lightweight dumbbell, increase the reps (13-15).

3. Extend your arms all the way down before explosively curling up.

4. This is great for targetting the inner bicep for size.

5. Negatives (4 SETS / 6-8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a moderate weight dumbbell for this.

2. If you only have a lightweight dumbbell, increase the reps (13-15).

3. Bring the weight all the way up before slowly letting it down (3-5 SEC).

4. Great for emphasizing growth in the biceps.

5. Keep your elbows tucked to your sides.

6. Hammer x Bicep Curls (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. This is a hybrid workout - each rep is a combination of each exercise to make for one rep.

2. Use a moderate weight dumbbell for this.

3. Make sure your elbow is tucked to your side.

4. Lower the weights down completely and back up until you fully squezee your bicep muscle.

5. This is great for building the inner and the outer bicep as well as the forearms.

7. Concentration Curls (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. This is great for isolating the bicep muscle for growth.

2. Use a lightweight dumbbell and then work your way up.

3. Rest your elbow on your thigh and make sure it remains in the same place the entire time.

4. Curl until you feel a squezee in your bicep muscle.

5. Maintain the same position throughout.
