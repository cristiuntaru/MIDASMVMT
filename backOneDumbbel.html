Back Single Dumbbell Workout

Looking for a Single Dumbbell BACK Workout at Home?

- Try this workout with only one dumbbell for the back muscles like the lats, the traps, and the rhomboids.

- This workout can be done at home, at the gym, or anywhere with no bench needed!

We have 7 exercises:

1. Leaning Side Rows

2. Leaning Hammer Rows

3. Leaning Neutral Rows

4. Rear Delt Flys

5. Front Shrugs

6. Leaning Rear Delt Flys

7. Reverse Shrugs
1. Leaning Side Rows (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8 reps/arm. Lightweight do 12-15 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell along your side while leaning forward with arm fully extended.

3. Use your other hand for support to prevent you from falling over.

4. Keep your body stationary. Row the dumbbell along your side.

5. This one focuses on training your lats.

6. Row the dumbbell as far as you can or until you feel your back work.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

2. Leaning Hammer Rows (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8 reps. Lightweight do 12-15 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell straight down while leaning forward with arms fully extended.

3. Lift dumbbell until it reaches your ab region and your elbows point outward.

4. Keep your body stationary. Row the dumbbell along the center of your body.

5. This one focuses on training your rhomboids.

6. Row the dumbbell as far as you can or until you feel your back squeeze.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

3. Leaning Neutral Rows (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 8 reps. Lightweight do 12-15 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at both ends straight down while leaning forward with arms fully extended.

3. Lift dumbbell until it reaches your ab region and your elbows point outward.

4. Keep your body stationary. Row the dumbbell along the center of your body.

5. This one focuses on training your entire upper back.

6. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

4. Rear Delt Flys (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Start by holding the dumbbell in a 90 degree angle in front of you.

2. Twist the dumbbell outward from the centre while maintaining the position of your body throughout.

3. Use your other hand for support.

4. Maintain the same position throughout. Don't move any other part of your body.

5. This one focuses on training your traps and rear delts.

6. Twist the dumbbell as far back as you can or until you feel the tension.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

5. Front Shrugs (4 SETS / 15 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 15 reps. Lightweight do 20-30 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at both ends with arms fully extended and head tilted upwards.

3. Shrug/Lift the dumbbell with your shoulders while using arms as hook only.

4. Keep your body stationary. Shrug the dumbbell along the center of your body.

5. This one focuses on training the traps.

6. Hold the weight up for one second before letting it down.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

6. Leaning Rear Delt Flys (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Start by leaning forward and holding dumbbell in front of you with elbow slightly bent.

2. Flare the dumbbell out as far as possible while maintaining the same position of your body throughout.

3. Use your other hand for support.

4. This one focuses on training your traps, lats and rear delts.

5. Flare the dumbbell out until you feel the tension.

6. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

7. Reverse Shrugs (4 SETS / 15 REPS)
TIPS:

1. If you have a heavy dumbbell, do 15 reps. Lightweight do 20-30 reps.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at the centre with arm fully extended and head tilted upwards.

3. Shrug/Lift the dumbbell with your shoulders while using arms as hook only.

4. Make sure you're holding the weight with both hands behind your back.

5. This focuses on training the traps.

6. Hold the weight up for one second before letting it down.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.
