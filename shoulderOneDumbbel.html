Shoulder Single Dumbbell Workout

Looking for a Single Dumbbell SHOULDER or DELT Workout at Home?

- Try this workout with one dumbbell for the shoulder muscles like the Front Delts, Side Delts, and Rear Delts.

- This workout can be done at home, at the gym, or anywhere with no bench needed!

We have 7 exercises:

1. Shoulder Press

2. Pronated x Hammer Front Raises

3. Double Front Raises

4. Side Raises

5. Rear Delt Side Raises

6. Twist Raises
1. Shoulder Press (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell out by your head. Make sure your start with dumbbell lowered.

3. Push the dumbbell upward while maintaining the position of your body until elbow straightens out.

4. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

5. Lower the dumbbell until your elbow is fully bent or until you can't lower it anymore.

6. This focuses on training your side delts/shoulders.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

2. Pronated x Hammer Front Raises (4 SETS / 8 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a lightweight dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell in front of your leg.

3. Lift the dumbbell upwards with your delts/shoulders until just past shoulder level.

4. Twist the dumbbell into a hammer grip and repeat the same action.

5. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

6. Keep elbows only slightly bent as you lift the dumbbell upwards.

7. This focuses on training your front delts/shoulders.

3. Double Front Raises (4 SETS / 12 REPS)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell at both ends right in front of your body.

3. Raise the dumbbell upwards till it is just past your shoulder level and you feel it in your delts.

4. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

5. Keep elbows slightly bent as you lift the dumbbell upwards.

6. This focuses on training your front delts/shoulders.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

4. Side Raises (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell out along one side of your body.

3. Lift the dumbbell outwards along one side until just past shoulder level and you feel it in your delts.

4. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

5. Keep elbows slightly bent as you lift the dumbbell upwards.

6. This focuses on training your side delts/shoulders.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

5. Rear Delt Side Raises (4 SETS / 12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell in front of one leg.

3. Lift the dumbbell outwards along one side until just past shoulder level and you feel it in your delts.

4. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

5. Keep elbows slightly bent as you lift the dumbbell upwards.

6. This focuses on training your rear delts/shoulders.

7. Take 45 seconds to 1 minute break between each set.

6. Twist Raises (4 SETS / 8-12 REPS/ARM)
TIPS:

1. Use a light to moderate dumbbell for this. Increase as you get stronger.

2. Start by holding the dumbbell with a supinated grip in front of one leg.

3. Lift the dumbbell upwards until just past shoulder level and feel it in your delts.

4. With dumbbell lifted, hold for one second and twist to a pronated grip before letting it down.

5. Keep your body stationary and move the working arm only.

6. Keep elbows slightly bent as you lift the dumbbell upwards.

7. This focuses on training your front/side delts/shoulders.
